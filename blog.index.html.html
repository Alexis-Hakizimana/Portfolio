SHORT INTRO

I’m Alexis Hakizimana, a long-life learner, and an ambitious, maybe an overambitious guy. I am always questioning certain aspects of myself and everything around me, and it does help me find different topics I can write about both for fun, for self-exploration, and also to understand and relate with the world around me.

As a student who has chased and achieved educational opportunities such as securing a highly competitive fully funded bachelor’s program in a US school under a 3% admission rate, I believe that with proper guidance and access to credible sources of information, I can help more students achieve the dreams they look far away from them. Therefore, I have created this blog to establish a platform where I can share up-to-date educational opportunities, and also other important opportunities to benefit students at any level especially secondary school students and graduates who want to pursue undergraduate studies, bachelor’s students and graduates who want to pursue master’s students, master’s students and graduates who want to pursue PhD. Most importantly, I have established this blog to connect with students who need support as they navigate their academic career and applications. Therefore, please reach out to me. I am more than happy to help and can’t wait to connect with you.

WHY SEEK HELP FROM ME?

  • Applications are usually daunting due to heavy writing required which goes beyond just meeting the word-limit. Thus, as a student who has and is still engaged in the intense-writing and reading US liberal arts education curriculum, I am strongly positioned to write well on any topic, and prepare a range of documents including essays (long and short, personal statements, supplemental essays,...), statement of purpose, research paper, research proposal, letter of intent, CV/Resume, and other different types of writings. I am more than happy to help more students uplevel their writing to increase their competitiveness for admission in top Colleges and Universities.
  • Having better grades is invaluable, but having someone who’s been in your shoes guide you is paramount. Usually top grades do not translate into admissions. Guidance can though.
  • I have applied to different places including the United States, Africa, Canada, Europe, Asia, and Australia among others and it has equipped me with a lot of information about the application process, documents required and an understanding of what you can do to make your application stand out.
  • I have been under mentorship from experienced mentors and study group leaders in the Equity Leaders Program who have passed their skills with me regarding navigating college application, career pathway and college life. I would love to share the skills I have honed through the ELP program.
